Pablo Muñoz-Rodríguez

Ramón y Cajal Researcher

Universidad Complutense de Madrid

I am a scientist working on the taxonomy and systematics of poorly known tropical plant groups, mainly morning glories (Ipomoea, Convolvulaceae) and Acalypha (Euphorbiaceae), with a focus on the origin and evolution of the sweet potato and other plants of economic interest 🌱. My work integrates taxonomy with molecular phylogenomics, aiming to better understand the world's biodiversity. I lead a small research team at Universidad Complutense de Madrid, Spain, focused on the taxonomy and systematics of several plant groups. Collaborating with researchers worldwide, we work not only in plant taxonomy but also across disciplines that enhance our understanding and conservation of the natural world.

Good taxonomy is necessary for conserving the world’s biological diversity. In the face of climate change and the ongoing biodiversity crisis, we must accelerate the study of life on Earth, describe new species more efficiently, and establish a robust taxonomic framework to integrate existing knowledge.

The science, the pain-staking taxonomy of identifying and recording many known specimens in a species and creating a monograph, is unadulterated, hard-core botany. It may not be good TV, but it is fundamental and good science.

Research Interests

  • 🧬
    Plant Systematics, Taxonomy & Phylogenomics

    Using DNA sequencing and morphology to untangle evolutionary histories and species boundaries in megadiverse plant genera.

  • 🍠
    Crop Evolution & Domestication

    Investigating how plants of economic importance such as sweet potato originated, evolved, and spread across the Globe.

  • 📊
    Plant Uses & Biodiversity Data

    Integrating a robust taxonomy with global biodiversity databases to identify evolutionary patterns, optimise conservation guidelines and improve species knowledge.

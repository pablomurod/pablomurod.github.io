Biography

I am a scientist working on the taxonomy and systematics of poorly known tropical plant groups, mainly morning glories (Ipomoea, Convolvulaceae) and Acalypha (Euphorbiaceae), with a focus on the origin and evolution of the sweet potato and other plants of economic interest 🌱.

My work integrates traditional botanical taxonomy with molecular phylogenomics and interactive software design, aiming to make complex species identification accessible, dynamic, and computational.

💡 Interactive Keys: I develop dynamic diagnostic systems—such as the Acalypha Species Identification System—to help researchers and enthusiasts resolve plant identification bottlenecks instantly.

Research Interests

  • 🧬
    Plant Systematics, Phylogenomics & Taxonomy

    Using DNA sequencing and classic morphology to untangle evolutionary histories in complex plant genera.

  • 🍠
    Crop Evolution & Domestication

    Investigating how crops like sweet potato originated, evolved, and spread across the globe.

  • 📊
    Plant Uses & Biodiversity Data

    Synthesizing large-scale distribution data to optimize conservation guidelines and taxonomy systems.
